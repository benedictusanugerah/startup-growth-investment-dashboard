# Startup Growth & Investment Dashboard (2000–2023)

## 📌 Project Overview
An interactive Excel dashboard that explores startup funding and valuation dynamics from **2000–2023** across industries and countries. It consolidates key signals **investment, valuation, growth rate, and ecosystem activity** to help users compare markets, spot momentum shifts, and extract actionable investment themes.

## 🎯 Business Objective
Support investment decisions by identifying **3–5 priority sectors** and **5–10 target markets** using growth signals from **2000–2023**, including **funding and valuation trends, country growth rates, and startup/investor participation**.

## 🛠 Tools & Dataset
- **Microsoft Excel:** Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, KPI Cards, Slicers  
- **Dataset:** Startup Growth & Investment Dataset (Kaggle)

## 📊 Key Insights
- Funding and valuation move in tandem over time, suggesting a positive relationship between capital inflow and company valuation.
- **HealthTech, SaaS, and E-Commerce** stand out as priority sectors based on total investment and valuation trends.
- **USA and Germany** emerge as high-priority markets with strong average growth and ecosystem signals.
- Capital allocation is concentrated in a small set of industries even when startup distribution is relatively broad.
- Ecosystem depth (startup count and investor participation) provides an investability signal beyond growth alone.

## ✅ Strategic Recommendations
- Focus screening on **3 core sectors** (plus a small watchlist) to improve pipeline efficiency.
- Shortlist **5–10 markets** by growth rate, then validate using startup and investor participation.
- Use a valuation-to-investment benchmark to flag potentially overheated segments and manage pricing risk.
- Use slicers (Industry × Country × Year Founded) to identify high-growth pockets and turn insights into an actionable shortlist.
